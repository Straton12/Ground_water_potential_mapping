Executive Summary of Findings

Integrated Analytical Framework

This study successfully develops and demonstrates an integrated analytical framework that combines water infrastructure monitoring with hydrogeological assessment, providing comprehensive insights for evidence-based water resource management in Kenya.

Key Achievement 1

Development of multi-page Power BI dashboard integrating spatial infrastructure data with geological assessment.

Key Achievement 2

Successful application of GIS, remote sensing, and AHP methodology for groundwater potential mapping.

Key Achievement 3

Identification of critical infrastructure gaps and geological constraints affecting water access sustainability.

Immediate Actions (0-3 Months)

Priority recommendations for immediate implementation to address critical gaps

1
High Priority

Technology Audit & Documentation

Conduct comprehensive assessment of 5,900 unknown technology installations to enable proper maintenance planning and spare parts provisioning.

Action Items:
Establish audit team

Form multidisciplinary team including hydrogeologists, engineers, and data specialists

Develop audit protocol

Create standardized checklist for technology classification and documentation

Pilot in 3 counties

Implement audit in Nairobi, Kiambu, and Marsabit counties

2
High Priority

Rehabilitation Prioritization

Develop targeted rehabilitation program for non-functional water points in high-population areas with favorable geological conditions.

Action Items:
Identify priority areas

Use dashboard to identify high-impact non-functional points

Allocate resources

Secure funding and materials for rehabilitation program

Community engagement

Involve local communities in rehabilitation planning and implementation

3
Medium Priority

Data Standardization

Implement improved data collection protocols with standardized technology classification, status reporting, and geological context documentation.

Action Items:
Develop standards

Create national water point data collection standards

Training programs

Train county water officers on new data collection protocols

Digital tools

Develop mobile data collection applications

Medium-Term Development (3-12 Months)

Strategic initiatives for sustainable water resource development

1

Geological Targeting

Months 4-6

Focus new water development in areas with moderate to high groundwater potential identified through GIS-AHP analysis, avoiding low-potential zones without specialized approaches.

  • Integrate geological maps into county water planning
  • Train drilling contractors on geological assessment
  • Develop site selection guidelines based on potential zones
  • Establish monitoring system for new developments
2

Technology Matching

Months 7-9

Align water development technologies with geological characteristics: motorized systems for high-yield areas, manual systems for low-yield areas, specialized equipment for fractured rock.

  • Develop technology selection guidelines
  • Create maintenance protocols for each technology type
  • Establish spare parts supply chains
  • Train community maintenance teams
3

Capacity Building

Months 10-12

Strengthen local management capabilities through training programs, spare parts supply chains, and community-based maintenance systems for sustainable operations.

  • Develop training curriculum for water management
  • Establish county-level training centers
  • Create certification program for water technicians
  • Develop community management models

Long-Term Planning (1-3 Years)

Strategic initiatives for sustainable water resource development and climate resilience

1

Integrated Water Management

Develop county-level water master plans incorporating geological data, climate projections, population trends, and infrastructure mapping for holistic resource management.

2

Climate Resilience

Design water infrastructure considering climate change impacts on water resources, incorporating adaptive capacity, drought resilience, and flood protection measures.

3

Community Engagement

Strengthen community participation in water point management through ownership models, decision-making inclusion, and benefit-sharing mechanisms.

Policy Development

  • National guidelines for geological integration in water planning
  • Standards for climate-resilient water infrastructure
  • Regulations for sustainable groundwater extraction
  • Framework for public-private partnerships

Institutional Strengthening

  • Capacity building at county water departments
  • Establishment of water resource monitoring units
  • Development of decision support systems
  • Inter-agency coordination mechanisms

Financing Mechanisms

  • Blended financing for sustainable water infrastructure
  • Results-based financing for rehabilitation
  • Community-managed water funds
  • Climate adaptation financing
